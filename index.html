About Our Team

Empower physical engineering and technology leadership since 2018

Our Rich History

FRC Team 9999 was formed in 2018 by five pioneering students and two faculty mentors in a small storage locker behind the physics laboratory. Operating with borrowed hand tools and a minuscule budget, the original team designed a wood-framed robot that surprised the regional competition, earning the "Highest Rookie Seed" award.

Over the last several years, the team has expanded to over 40 active students, supported by professional engineering mentors, parents, and school administrators. Today, we work inside a dedicated technology workshop, utilising 3D printing, laser cutters, and CNC machinery to craft professional-grade robotics. Despite our technological expansion, we preserve our early founding principles: welcoming any student regardless of prior knowledge and operating as a tight-knit family.

Venturi STEM Summer Camp

Every July, FRC Team 9999 hosts a two-week STEM immersion camp for middle school students in our school district. Led entirely by senior high school students, the campers discover mechanical assembly using LEGO Mindstorms, and explore basic software logic using Python.

Our mission is to make technology education highly accessible. Over 60% of our attendees receive full camp scholarships funded directly by our Gold sponsors, ensuring cost is never a barrier to starting a STEM journey.

Elementary Demonstration Visits

We visit four local elementary schools every spring to demonstrate our competition robot. Students interact with the mechanics, operate the intake controls, and learn about elementary kinetic principles from our software team.

E-Waste Recycling Initiative

To promote environmental stewardship alongside engineering, our team organizes bi-monthly community e-waste collection drives. We have safely recycled over 15,000 lbs of toxic hardware since starting in 2021.

Venturi Effect Boosters

The parent and community coalition fueling our logistics and operations

Who We Are

The Venturi Effect Booster Club is a non-profit organization made up of parents, guardians, alumni, and local supporters dedicated to assisting FRC Team 9999. While the mentors coordinate technical concepts and the students construct the robot, the Boosters coordinate the vital operations that keep our gears turning:

Pit Catering & Nutrition

Coordination of nutritional lunch boxes and healthy snacks to keep the students fully energized during long build sessions and high-stakes district events.

Event Travel & Lodging

Assisting faculty with the logistics of transporting 40 students, tool cabinets, and robot containers safely to local and international competitions.

Merchandise & Brand

Sourcing quality team apparel, banners, badges, and promotional items for students and parent blocks in the tournament stands.

End of Season Celebrations

Planning and organizing our celebrated annual team banquets, awarding achievements, and honoring our graduating student seniors.

About FIRST®

For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology

The Ultimate Sport for the Mind

**FIRST®** (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is a global robotics community that prepares young people for the future through a suite of inclusive, team-based robotics programs for schools and communities.

Under strict rules and limited resources, teams of high school students are challenged to raise funds, design a team "brand," hone teamwork skills, and build and program industrial-size robots to play a difficult field game against like-minded competitors. It’s as close to real-world engineering as a student can get.

Gracious Professionalism®

A way of doing things that encourages high-quality work, emphasizes the value of others, and respects individuals and the community. Through Gracious Professionalism, fierce competition and mutual gain can coexist. We compete hard on the field, but actively help other teams repair their robots in the pits.

Coopertition®

Coopertition® produces innovation by promoting collaboration and respect in the face of intense competition. It means assisting and enabling teammates, competitors, and opponents, even as you strive to win. It teaches that helping others is a core component of outstanding engineering.
